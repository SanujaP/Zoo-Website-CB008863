History and Orgin of National Zoological Gardens Sri Lanka

National Zoological Gardens of Sri Lanka was founded in early 1920s by John Hagenbeck. The zoo was again closed during the wolrd war two and reopened in 1936 under the name "Zoological Garden of Ceylon".

The Zoo's first Director was Major Aurbey Neil Weinman, which during his period did an excellent job by developing the zoo by introducing new programs, more native and foreign species for the collection and improving the fcailities and infrastructure of the zoo.
